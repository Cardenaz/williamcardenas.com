### What can we learn from St. Augustine?

![augustine](/images/augustine.png)

The first good thing about Augustine is that he was *not* a skeptic, i.e., he defended the possibility of *knowledge*. But what struck me is that he did not do it solely on logical grounds but on moral ones. What does that mean? For Augustine, "happiness" seems to be the goal, and since the skeptic by his own construction is someone who searches without any possibility of discovery, he cannot be happy. In fact, we would say that he is rather silly.
